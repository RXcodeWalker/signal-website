2-1 win at the Emirates for Arsenal.

Finally, Arsenal have shortened the gap to Liverpool to 4 points and capitalized on an occasion where Liverpool dropped points, winning the North London derby 2-1 at home following Liverpool's 1-1 draw against Nottingham Forest. Liverpool still have a game in hand, but we have edged two points closer this week.

<strong>Relentless Build-Up, Poor Finishing</strong>

It was yet another instance where we created various chances but seemed to overplay them all as we couldn't get the right shot. It felt like we were trying too hard to find the perfect finish, which led to too many touches in the final third. We should have finished Tottenham off from the start with at least a two-goal margin, especially considering our first goal was an own goal from a corner kick that arguably should have been a goal kick. If not for that, I can imagine Arsenal drawing against a fragile, injury-hit Spurs side, which would have been a massive disappointment.

Our attack was more relentless and fluid than our weekend fixture against Manchester United, which is an improvement. Once again, we dominated possession and chances.

And once again, we struggled to capitalize.

Therefore...

<strong>We Need a Striker</strong>

Havertz didn't play well; his finishing was below par even though he worked hard and pressed effectively. Trossard stepped up with a magnificent finish, far sharper than many of our recent goals. That's how we should be converting our chances if we want to challenge Liverpool for the title.

<strong>Rock-Solid Defence</strong>

Raya made some key saves, particularly when he denied Kulusevski following a set piece. He also came out confidently to collect several high crosses before they could cause danger.

Timber kept Son quiet, Saliba and Gabriel were solid, and Myles Lewis-Skelly was fantastic. The 18-year-old defended brilliantly and deservedly received a standing ovation from the home crowd when he was substituted for Zinchenko. Gabriel also caused problems from corners and took a strong header that deflected into the goal.

It was another excellent performance from the back five.

<strong>Strong Midfield</strong>

Declan Rice delivered a remarkable performance. He started slowly but grew into the game, making powerful dribbles through the final third and controlling midfield with authority. Odegaard showed his usual elegance in movement and link-up play, though he still seems slightly below his peak level since Saka's hamstring injury. Partey gave the ball away a couple of times but still performed adequately overall.

<strong>Lingering Attacking Issues</strong>

Trossard scored well, but he was the only forward who truly impressed. Sterling was too passive and failed to make the most of his first Premier League start this season. He was replaced by Martinelli at the hour mark, but Martinelli is less effective on the right than on his natural left side. Nwaneri is expected to return later this month and has been the most capable option in Saka's absence, so his return could be crucial. Havertz worked tirelessly but once again lacked clinical finishing.

<strong>Conclusion</strong>

All in all, it was another trademark Arsenal performance: fantastic defence, strong midfield control, promising build-up play, but poor finishing. On the bright side, we won a London derby and gained two crucial points on Liverpool.
