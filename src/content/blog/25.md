Today, I attended the first session of the five-day Harvard YLC (Youth Lead the Change) leadership conference, in which Harvard students came to India to teach us about leadership. The opening hour was dedicated to exploring and defining the broad concept of leadership, and through discussion we identified several core characteristics and responsibilities that effective leaders should embody.

<strong>The Role of a Leader</strong>

A leader’s responsibility is not to control, but to guide and elevate a team. That includes maintaining discipline and direction, ensuring the group remains aligned with its objectives, and creating a culture of accountability.

Leaders must encourage and motivate their team to perform at their highest potential. They should accept responsibility for collective outcomes, ensure every voice is heard, and stand up for their team when necessary.

Strong leadership also demands persistence, resilience, and the willingness to take calculated risks. Decisions should be rooted in logic, reasoning, and evidence rather than impulse or emotion. At the same time, leaders must understand the needs and aspirations of their team members, ensuring that individuals feel supported and comfortable — because empowered individuals perform at their best.

These qualities strengthen team dynamics, improve collaboration, and increase the likelihood of meaningful success.

Ultimately, the most important realization from today’s session was this: the true purpose of leadership is not to exert control, but to guide, inspire, and empower others toward a shared vision.
