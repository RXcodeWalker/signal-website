Arsenal have signed Spanish CDM Martin Zubimendi for 51 million pounds. Zubimendi, aged 25, is set to join Arsenal from Real Sociedad over the summer. Unfortunately, this means that we won't have him in our team until next season. Nevertheless, it is a fantastic signing on the face of it.

<strong>Midfield Transition</strong>

Thomas Partey and Jorginho, both of whom are over 30 years old, seem set to leave Arsenal once their contracts expire at the end of the season. Jorginho was never worth a lot but Partey was signed for 45 million pounds and has since been a pivotal central midfielder for us. However, Zubimendi has the advantage in his youth, agility, and defensive capabilities, which are only expected to improve as he shifts from a 7th-place La Liga side to a dominant team like Arsenal, who are challenging for the Premier League title.

This is a testament to the progress that Arteta has made ever since joining Arsenal, despite the growing number of disgruntled fans leading the ArtetaOut movement. Many people have been demanding why Arsenal are investing in a CDM rather than a striker, who they well and truly require.

<strong>Why This Signing Makes Sense</strong>

However, getting Zubimendi will complement Odegaard and Rice to add more quality to the midfield, and it will only strengthen our squad next season. As mentioned, Zubimendi is arguably an upgrade on both Jorginho and Partey at this stage, and signing him enhances the depth and longevity of our squad.

This showcases our long-term mentality and how we are continuously improving our team. After Arsenal lost to Manchester United, we seemed to be falling apart. The club should have signed a striker after the summer transfer window last season, but they didn't.

<strong>Transfer Window Criticism</strong>

Arteta takes a fair share of the blame for this, which he deserves. His stubbornness does annoy me at times, especially his optimism surrounding Havertz and Jesus, both of whom have been below par -- not to mention that Jesus will be out for another 6-12 months as he recovers from an ACL injury.

With Nketiah and Nelson leaving Arsenal, and injuries to Saka, Nwaneri, and Jesus, we've effectively lost five forwards. The only reinforcement has been a declining Sterling who is yet to start in a Premier League game. Trossard, Martinelli, and Havertz have also underperformed, with Havertz by far the most inconsistent of the three.

However, a lot of criticism unfairly gets directed at Arteta. Some claims, such as sacking him, are absolutely ridiculous given the progress we have made. Although he does not have extensive silverware -- just an FA Cup and a Community Shield in his tenure -- he has transformed us from a struggling mid-table team into a dominant, title-contending side that comfortably qualifies for the Champions League.

<strong>Backing the Manager</strong>

That is why, although he should have done a better job in the summer transfer window and can be stubborn at times, Arteta is still a great manager who has executed a strong rebuild. In moments when the team is struggling -- such as now -- the club and fanbase must stay calm and avoid overreaction. Despite the absence of a Sporting Director following Edu Gaspar's departure last year, Arteta and the management have secured a high-quality player for next season.

Arteta has also mentioned that the club is actively searching the transfer market for further reinforcements. Rumours have emerged regarding a bid for Dusan Vlahovic and interest in both Benjamin Sesko and Viktor Gyokeres. All this highlights how premature the ArtetaOut movement truly is -- we don't even have a clear alternative in mind.

<strong>Conclusion</strong>

All in all, it's a strong move from Arteta that defies the criticism surrounding him. Zubimendi will add quality and depth to our midfield, and it shows that the club has long-term plans in place despite current setbacks.
