In Gameweek 23, Arsenal beat relegation-battling Wolves at the Molineux, securing three points and a clean sheet with a 1-0 win. Finally, we won with 10 men. This keeps the gap between us and Liverpool level as they enter a double gameweek against Bournemouth and Everton.

<strong>Controversial Red Card</strong>

Initially, we weren't attacking with enough intensity. Two minutes before halftime, Myles Lewis-Skelly clipped a Wolves player who was about to launch a counterattack near their box. Michael Oliver deemed it a "serious challenge" and sent the 18-year-old off, a decision many fans and pundits felt only warranted a yellow. VAR chose not to intervene beyond confirming the decision.

We were forced to play the remainder of the match with 10 men.

After the restart, Oliver did not give Gomes a second yellow for kicking the ball away. Later, instead of awarding a straight red for a harsher challenge on Timber, he issued Gomes a second yellow. Substitute Calafiori capitalized soon after, scoring the decisive goal minutes following the equalizer in numbers. We ultimately won the 10v10 battle.

<strong>Defensive Resilience</strong>

Despite the numerical disadvantage, our defence held firm. Wolves created a few chances after the hour mark, but Raya and the backline remained composed. We secured our second consecutive clean sheet.

Fun fact: the last time we won back-to-back games with back-to-back clean sheets was our opening fixtures against Wolves and Villa in August. Defensive solidity has not been as consistent this season as last.

<strong>Midfield Performance</strong>

Partey delivered a smooth display, and Rice continued his strong form following his first Champions League goal. Nwaneri had a decent outing filling in for Odegaard, who missed the game due to illness. Though naturally more suited to the right wing, he performed adequately in midfield.

<strong>Attacking Concerns</strong>

Martinelli was arguably our best forward despite playing on the right instead of his preferred left flank. Trossard was less creative than in recent games but still serviceable. Havertz struggled again, missing two key first-half chances and reinforcing concerns about our lack of a clinical striker.

<strong>Super-Sub Impact</strong>

Calafiori came on at halftime and scored with a composed left-footed volley. It was his second Arsenal goal, the first being a stunning strike against Manchester City. His contribution secured a crucial victory.

<strong>Looking Ahead</strong>

Next, we host Manchester City. Despite their struggles and recent defeat to PSG, players like Foden and new signing Marmoush remain threats. City are improving and will test us.

Hopefully, our appeal for the Lewis-Skelly red card succeeds so he can feature against City. He has been exceptional at left back and would be a valuable option, whether starting or off the bench.
