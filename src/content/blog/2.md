Last season, Arsenal drew 2-2 with Manchester City after leading 2-1 before Trossard’s red card forced us to defend deep with ten men, only for John Stones to equalise in stoppage time. This time, the script flipped. Haaland opened the scoring again, but City protected their lead with an ultra-defensive 5-5-0 shape. Just when it seemed enough, Gabriel Martinelli struck late to level it. A poetic coincidence — yet from Arsenal’s perspective, it felt like two more points dropped.

<strong>Team Selection Questions</strong>

Arteta’s lineup raised eyebrows immediately. Merino started as a number ten ahead of Nwaneri. Trossard was preferred over Eze. Against Manchester City, with title implications looming, the setup felt cautious rather than assertive. If Arsenal aim to win the Premier League, the approach must be proactive — aggressive in possession and ruthless in attack.

Instead, the starting structure limited creativity. Merino, naturally more defensive, struggled to unlock space. Trossard lacked directness. The issue was not individual effort, but tactical intent.

<strong>A Pattern Emerging</strong>

This mirrored the approach at Anfield weeks earlier, where conservative choices allowed Liverpool to dictate rhythm. Beginning high-stakes matches with a safety-first mindset often hands initiative to elite opponents. Arsenal’s recurring issue is converting control into goals. Playing not to lose only amplifies that weakness.

Arteta’s substitutions also invited scrutiny. Merino remained until halftime, Trossard until the 80th minute. The urgency to shift momentum felt delayed. While Martinelli’s equaliser showcased resilience, it masked a broader concern about tactical boldness.

<strong>No More Excuses</strong>

This summer’s recruitment has strengthened the squad significantly. Defensive depth, midfield reinforcement, and attacking upgrades with Eze, Gyokeres, and Madueke provide genuine firepower. Arsenal now possess the tools required to compete at the highest level.

With that depth comes expectation.

For three consecutive seasons, Arsenal have been runners-up. The squad is no longer transitional — it is competitive and mature. This may be a defining campaign for Arteta. Big games require conviction, not hesitation.

<strong>The Mentality Shift</strong>

Arteta has elevated Arsenal from mid-table instability to consistent title contention. That achievement deserves respect. But to take the final step, the team must approach decisive matches with controlled aggression rather than caution.

In my view, a more attacking setup — Nwaneri over Merino, Eze over Trossard — could have tilted the match. Arsenal’s squad is capable of dominating elite opposition. The question now is whether the tactical mindset will match that potential.

The pieces are in place.

Now it is about belief, boldness, and execution.
