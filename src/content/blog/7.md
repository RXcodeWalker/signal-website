THIS IS THE MOMENT WE ARSENAL FANS DREAM OF!

Arsenal just beat Real Madrid 3-0. Unbelievable. Declan Rice scored two sensational free kicks, and I have been replaying them ever since -- especially the second one.

<strong>Context Before the Explosion</strong>

Recent performances had been underwhelming. January passed without reinforcements despite mounting injuries and shallow squad depth. Results felt flat. The return of Bukayo Saka offered hope, but Gabriel's season-ending hamstring injury quickly dampened optimism.

Facing 15-time Champions League winners Real Madrid without Gabriel felt daunting. With Kiwior expected to face Bellingham, Vinicius Jr, and Mbappe, confidence was limited.

I was wrong. And I am glad.

<strong>Total Control</strong>

Arsenal registered 11 shots on target and controlled the match from start to finish. Real Madrid survived early pressure thanks to goal-line clearances and outstanding saves from Courtois.

<strong>Declan Rice's Historic Night</strong>

Near the hour mark, Rice ignored the set-piece instruction to chip the ball and instead curled a stunning effort from distance. It was pure artistry -- reminiscent of classic Brazilian technique blended with English precision.

Critics questioned Madrid's wall setup, but the strike was unstoppable. Courtois, despite being their best player, had no chance.

Then Rice did it again. An even more precise free kick into the top-right corner. Only a handful of players have scored two direct free kicks in a Champions League match over the past twenty-five years. This was historic.

<strong>Merino's Evolution</strong>

Merino added a third with a composed finish from the edge of the box. His transformation from defensive midfielder to effective false nine continues to impress. While a world-class striker remains a summer priority, he has delivered admirably in the interim.

Camavinga was sent off late for dissent, compounding Madrid's frustration. Arteta tactically outclassed Ancelotti. Even Bellingham admitted Madrid were fortunate it was only three.

<strong>Half-Time, Not Full-Time</strong>

This was a statement performance, but the tie is only halfway complete. Real Madrid remain dangerous, particularly at the Santiago Bernabeu. However, Arsenal's defensive record provides confidence.

The second leg awaits.
