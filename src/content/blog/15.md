Yesterday, we drew 2-2 with Aston Villa at home, marking our 8th draw of the season after bottling a 2-0 lead just before the hour mark. With Darwin Nunez's stoppage-time brace securing Liverpool a 2-0 win against Brentford, Arsenal are once again 6 points behind Liverpool, who still have a game in hand.

Arsenal dominated the first half and took the lead as Martinelli converted a cross from Trossard that Martinez failed to clear in time. Ten minutes after the break, Havertz doubled the lead by tapping in another Trossard cross, scoring for the first time since 28th December against Ipswich Town.

However, Villa struck twice in eight minutes on the break to level the game. Arsenal pushed late, with a Merino goal disallowed by VAR for a Havertz handball. Minutes later, Merino hit the post and Trossard saw his rebound saved. In the dying moments, Timber slipped Trossard through 1v1 with Martinez, but his shot went narrowly wide. The game ended as a frustrating 2-2 draw.

<strong>Are Defensive Injuries to Blame?</strong>

We were cruising at 2-0 before conceding twice in eight minutes. The defence takes significant responsibility. Saliba suffered a hamstring injury which Arteta was "very worried" about, suggesting a potential absence. Timber replaced him alongside Gabriel and performed reasonably well.

With Ben White, Tomiyasu, and Calafiori unavailable, and Zinchenko and Kiwior unreliable at right back, Arteta kept Partey at right back. Unfortunately, Partey struggled defensively. He misthrew a ball that gifted Watkins a chance and later failed to track him for the equaliser. Timber or White would likely have handled it better.

Myles Lewis-Skelly impressed again with maturity beyond his years. Against a strong Villa side featuring Morgan Rogers, he showed composure and discipline. Still, conceding twice after dominating remains unacceptable.

<strong>Midfield Imbalance</strong>

Declan Rice controlled the midfield much like he did against Tottenham, driving forward and setting tempo. However, Odegaard and Merino failed to support him.

Odegaard has not returned to his innovative best since his ankle injury, worsened by Saka's absence. His creative spark feels diminished. As captain, he must rediscover that attacking sharpness.

Merino contributed little for most of the match. Though strong aerially, his touches are limited and his influence minimal. His late disallowed goal and shot off the post were promising, but overall he remains more suited to a bench role, especially once Zubimendi joins.

<strong>Attacking Improvements</strong>

Trossard stepped up significantly. He delivered two excellent assists and was dangerous throughout, though he missed the late winner. Hopefully, this signals a return to form.

Martinelli showed improvement on the right flank and made intelligent runs, including the opener. A positive sign.

Havertz scored but remained slow and ineffective overall. While the goal may boost confidence, we still need a true striker. Reports suggest RB Leipzig may accept a 70 million Euro bid for Benjamin Sesko, which could greatly strengthen our attack.

<strong>Conclusion</strong>

We bottled a 2-0 lead and dropped crucial points. Defensive instability following Saliba's injury hurt us. Rice performed strongly, Trossard improved, but Odegaard must step up and Havertz remains inconsistent. With Wolves away next, we desperately need stability and momentum.
